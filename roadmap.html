OneRNG

Open Hardware Random Number Generator

The OneRNG is a project to create a reliable and open hardware random number generator - one that can be verified by the user and can be trusted.

Project Timeline & Roadmap

Begin at the beginning …

At the end of November 2013 Jim Cheetham started the project, by getting in touch with a couple of trusted contacts to see if his idea for an Open solution for a hardware RNG was feasible.

Paul Campbell responsed enthuiastically, drafting hardware designs and proposing a base component set based on his recent projects and a recently-purchased pick'n'place machine.

Other contributions from people such as Ian Grigg and Peter Gutmann were more crypto based, and provided a great set of ideal requirements from their different perspectives. This helped us to realise at an early stage that we didn't need to be a fully independent RNG, but should leave the final stages to external software running in the OS. Sadly the project already had the name OneRNG and I didn't fancy "One ENT" as a replacement!

Public announcement

Jim was at LCA 2014 (linux.conf.au) in Perth in early January 2014, and filled in a vacant slot in the Open Programming miniconf with a quick (15 minutes over-running to 25!) presentation titled A Pseudo-Random Talk on Entropy. This had a brief intro to OneRNG but the audience didn't take the bait!

Sadly I'd missed another miniconf presentation on a HWRNG by Paul Warren, but got him onto our mailing list for future discussions.

I followed by announcing the OneRNG project to the whole LCA2014 community in the closing Lightning Talk session (at the 05:30 mark). My friend Dan at OnlineGroups.net contributed the mailing-list/forum hosting that we needed, and our project was off and running!

Beta Units

By April 2014 Paul had produced the first hand-build beta units, and we started distributing them to testers. By the end of July BadUSB became a thing, and validated Paul's decision to do firmware updating only over a dedicated port and not over USB at all!

Beta testing threw back a couple of broken devices, which helped to identify weak areas in the physical design, and a few OS use-case problems.

The Future Roadmap

Kiwicon 8 in Wellington, NZ will see the OneRNG on December 11th 2014.

Kickstarter will see OneRNG from Moonbase Otago hopefully on December 14th 2014.

LCA 2015 in Auckland, NZ will see OneRNG on January 15th 2015 - live stream video will probably be in place for that.

Kickstarter ...

Kickstarter will allow us to determine the global demand for this product, and give us a way to bridge the gap between hand-built units and small-scale automated production in China. Yes, China.

If the demand is high, Paul will happily spend time in Shenzen getting the best value components run through production, using project-specific testbed hardware to install the valid firmware images and confirm the sanity of the manufacturing process (checking voltages as well as USB communication and performance).

Now you see another reason for everyone to know how to validate their OneRNG devices; there is no totally secure supply-chain on the planet for the truly paranoid. If you know how to inspect the hardware and how to install your own firmware, you can mitigate so many more potential weaknesses in the devices that you need to rely on.
